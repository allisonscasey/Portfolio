Overview

This project investigated an engineering question through data. The goal was to move from raw information to a clear conclusion that someone else could evaluate, repeat, and use when making a decision.

The homepage card gives the short version. This page is designed for the deeper version: what the dataset contained, how it was cleaned, what assumptions mattered, and which visuals made the findings easiest to understand.

What I Did

  • Defined the analysis question and identified the data needed to answer it.
  • Cleaned, transformed, and checked the dataset for quality issues.
  • Built reproducible scripts or notebooks to make the analysis traceable.
  • Created visual summaries that communicated the findings clearly.

Process

I approached the analysis by making the assumptions explicit first. That helped keep the work honest: every chart, model, or conclusion had to connect back to what the data could actually support.

The most important part of the process was communication. I refined the visuals and explanations so that the final result did not depend on someone reading every line of code to understand the takeaway.
